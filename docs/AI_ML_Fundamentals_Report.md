# AI & Machine Learning Fundamentals Report

## Introduction

Artificial Intelligence (AI) is a branch of computer science that enables machines to perform tasks that normally require human intelligence, such as learning, reasoning, decision-making, and problem-solving. Machine Learning (ML) is a subset of AI that allows computers to learn from data without being explicitly programmed.

---

# What is Artificial Intelligence (AI)?

Artificial Intelligence is the simulation of human intelligence by machines. AI systems can analyze data, recognize patterns, make decisions, and improve their performance over time.

### Examples
- Virtual assistants (Siri, Alexa, Google Assistant)
- Self-driving cars
- Face recognition
- Chatbots

---

# What is Machine Learning (ML)?

Machine Learning is a branch of AI where computers learn from data and improve their predictions without explicit programming.

### Examples
- Email spam detection
- Movie recommendations
- Fraud detection
- Stock price prediction

---

# Difference Between AI and ML

| Artificial Intelligence | Machine Learning |
|-------------------------|------------------|
| Broader field | Subset of AI |
| Mimics human intelligence | Learns from data |
| Includes reasoning and planning | Focuses on prediction and learning |
| Can work with or without ML | Always part of AI |

---

# Types of Artificial Intelligence

## Narrow AI

Designed to perform one specific task.

Examples:
- Google Translate
- Face Unlock
- ChatGPT
- Alexa

## General AI

A future concept where machines can perform any intellectual task that humans can do. General AI has not yet been achieved.

---

# Types of Machine Learning

## Supervised Learning

Uses labeled data to train models.

Examples:
- House price prediction
- Student marks prediction

Common Algorithms:
- Linear Regression
- Decision Tree

---

## Unsupervised Learning

Finds hidden patterns in unlabeled data.

Examples:
- Customer segmentation
- Market basket analysis

Common Algorithms:
- K-Means Clustering
- PCA

---

## Reinforcement Learning

The model learns through rewards and penalties while interacting with an environment.

Examples:
- Game playing
- Robot navigation
- Self-driving cars

Common Algorithms:
- Q-Learning
- Deep Q Network (DQN)

---

# Conclusion

Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning are transforming industries by enabling smarter decision-making and automation. Understanding their concepts and applications forms the foundation for building intelligent systems in the future.
